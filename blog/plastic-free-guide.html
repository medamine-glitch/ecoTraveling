Plastic-Free Travel Guide: Morocco Edition

Published on May 10, 2024 by Amina Benali

Discover how to explore Morocco's vibrant cities and stunning landscapes while minimizing your plastic footprint. This comprehensive guide will help you navigate markets, accommodations, and daily life in Morocco without single-use plastics.

Essential Plastic-Free Travel Kit

Before embarking on your Moroccan adventure, pack these eco-friendly essentials:

  • Reusable water bottle with filter
  • Cloth shopping bags for souks and markets
  • Stainless steel or bamboo cutlery set
  • Reusable food containers for takeaway
  • Natural fiber shopping basket (or buy one locally)

Navigating Moroccan Markets

Morocco's vibrant markets offer excellent opportunities for plastic-free shopping:

  • Buy fresh produce directly from local vendors
  • Use traditional baskets ('znbil') for shopping
  • Purchase spices in bulk using your own containers
  • Choose fresh bread from traditional bakeries

Staying Hydrated Sustainably

Managing water consumption without plastic bottles:

  • Use water filtration systems in your accommodation
  • Locate safe drinking water refill stations
  • Consider UV water purification devices
  • Support establishments that offer filtered water

Eco-Friendly Accommodations

Choose accommodations that support plastic-free initiatives:

  • Riads and hotels with water filtration systems
  • Places offering refillable toiletry dispensers
  • Accommodations with composting and recycling programs
  • Establishments using traditional pottery instead of plastic

Dining Out Responsibly

Tips for plastic-free dining experiences:

  • Choose restaurants using traditional serving ware
  • Carry your own containers for leftovers
  • Opt for fresh juice in glass cups
  • Support establishments that avoid plastic straws

Common Challenges and Solutions

  • Challenge: Bottled water everywhere Solution: Carry a filter bottle and research safe refill stations
  • Challenge: Plastic bags at markets Solution: Keep a foldable cloth bag in your daypack
  • Challenge: Takeaway containers Solution: Carry a collapsible container for unexpected purchases
  • Challenge: Plastic-wrapped snacks Solution: Visit traditional bakeries and fresh fruit vendors
